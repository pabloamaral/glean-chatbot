BANKS & BANJO LLC PERFORMANCE REVIEWS & COMPENSATION GUIDE
Document ID: banks-banjo-hr-005
Last Updated: March 2025
Owner: People Operations

---

OVERVIEW

Banks & Banjo LLC runs a twice-yearly performance review cycle. The goals are to give employees clear, actionable feedback; recognize strong performance; and align compensation to impact. This document explains how the process works and what to expect.

---

REVIEW CYCLES

Banks & Banjo LLC holds two review cycles per year:

Mid-Year Review (July)
- Focused on feedback and development, not compensation changes.
- Includes a self-assessment, manager assessment, and optional peer feedback.
- Output: written feedback and updated goals for H2.

Annual Review (December/January)
- Full review cycle including self-assessment, manager assessment, peer feedback, and calibration.
- Tied to compensation adjustments (merit increases and bonuses) effective February 1.
- Output: written feedback, performance rating, and compensation update.

---

PERFORMANCE RATINGS

Banks & Banjo LLC uses a 5-level rating scale:

5 — Exceptional: Consistently exceeds all expectations; significant impact beyond scope of role.
4 — Exceeds Expectations: Frequently exceeds expectations; strong contributor.
3 — Meets Expectations: Consistently delivers on role requirements; solid performer.
2 — Partially Meets Expectations: Some gaps in performance; improvement plan may be initiated.
1 — Does Not Meet Expectations: Significant performance concerns; formal PIP likely.

The majority of employees (typically 60–70%) receive a rating of 3. Ratings of 4 and 5 are reserved for standout contributors.

---

PEER FEEDBACK

During each review cycle, employees nominate 3–5 peers to provide structured feedback. Managers may adjust the peer list. Peer feedback is:
- Anonymous to the employee receiving it.
- Visible to the employee's manager and HR.
- Summarized (not shared verbatim) in the written review.

Employees are also expected to provide peer feedback when nominated. Declining without a valid reason may be noted.

---

CALIBRATION

After managers submit assessments, all ratings are calibrated across teams by senior leaders and People Operations to ensure consistency and fairness across the organization. Calibration happens before results are shared with employees.

---

COMPENSATION

Merit Increases (Annual Review only):
- Employees rated 3 (Meets Expectations) are eligible for merit increases in line with the annual salary budget (typically 3–5%).
- Employees rated 4 or 5 receive above-target increases.
- Employees rated 2 or 1 are generally not eligible for merit increases.

Annual Bonus:
- Bonus eligibility and targets vary by role and level. Your offer letter specifies your target bonus percentage.
- Bonuses are calculated based on individual performance rating and company performance against annual goals.
- Bonuses are paid in February with the first payroll of the year.

Equity Refresh Grants:
- Employees rated 4 or 5 are considered for equity refresh grants during the Annual Review cycle.
- Refresh grants vest over 4 years with no cliff.

Salary Bands:
- Banks & Banjo LLC uses market-aligned salary bands for every role and level. Bands are reviewed annually.
- Employees can request to see their band by contacting their manager or People Operations.
- Banks & Banjo LLC targets the 65th percentile of market for base compensation.

---

PROMOTION

Promotions can happen at any review cycle (mid-year or annual) and are not limited to the review calendar for exceptional cases. Promotions require:
- Manager recommendation supported by clear performance evidence.
- Calibration approval by senior leadership.
- People Operations review for band and equity impact.

Employees interested in promotion should discuss criteria with their manager well in advance of the review cycle — typically at least one cycle before the target promotion date.

---

PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLANS (PIPs)

Employees rated 1 or 2 may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan. A PIP:
- Is a formal written document outlining specific gaps and measurable expectations.
- Runs for 30–90 days depending on the situation.
- Is managed jointly by the employee's manager and People Operations.

A PIP is an opportunity to course-correct, not a guaranteed precursor to termination. However, failure to meet PIP goals may result in separation.

TIMELINE (Annual Review)

November 15: Peer feedback due
November 22: Self-assessments due
December 6: Manager assessments due
December 9–20: Calibration sessions
January 10: Results communicated to employees
February 1: Compensation changes effective
February 15: Bonuses paid
